Background

How the work gets done

Good products come from getting close to the problem. Not reading about it in a report or hearing about it secondhand, but sitting with the person doing the work and watching where things break down. That has always been the starting point.

The habit came from years on the ground, in 18 states, training frontline teams on new software and seeing exactly where tools helped and where they fell short. That proximity became a practice: spend time with the people who will use the thing before designing anything, then stay close through every release.

Today that same principle applies at a larger scale. A 62-person global team across product, design, engineering, and operations. A $150M+ annual portfolio. Consumer apps, clinical tools, telehealth platforms, and enterprise workflow systems across 2,500+ locations. Some shipped in as little as 6 weeks. All rooted in understanding the real problem first.

AI Medical Dictation

Off-the-shelf transcription tools could not handle the complexity of veterinary medical records, so the team designed and built a custom dictation system from scratch. Clinicians speak, the system transcribes, and structured SOAP notes push directly into the practice management system. Cut documentation time by 90% and improved clinical efficiency by 30%. Went from concept to production in 6 weeks. Over 1.5M dictations processed so far. One of the first production AI tools in veterinary medicine, and it created real IP value for the organization.

ER & Urgent Care Operations

Virtual Waiting Room

No off-the-shelf queue system could handle the unpredictability of emergency veterinary care. So this was built from scratch. Entirely automated: clients check in digitally, get real-time wait estimates and status updates, and flow through to hospital reception without a phone call or lobby wait. Tens of thousands of phoneless placements and arrivals, designed to make a stressful situation as painless as possible. Deployed across 150+ urgent care and ER clinics. Created proprietary IP for the organization and drove a 20% revenue lift by keeping clients in the funnel.
